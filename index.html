Space debris, refers to man-made objects that are no longer functional or operational and are left orbiting in space. These objects can include spent rocket stages, defunct satellites, discarded spacecraft parts, and other debris resulting from human space activities.

Cosmic rays, high-energy particles that originate from various sources in space, including stars, supernovae, and other cosmic events. These particles travel at nearly the speed of light and can penetrate through the vacuum of space, as well as through planetary atmospheres and other matter.

Fun Fact

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, called Olympus Mons. It stands at a height of 22 kilometers (14 miles), making it almost three times taller than Mount Everest, the tallest mountain on Earth. Interestingly, Olympus Mons is also one of the youngest volcanoes on Mars, with its last eruption estimated to have occurred only about 25 million years ago, which is relatively recent in geological time.

Fun Fact

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the sun in our solar system. One interesting fact about Neptune is that it is the windiest planet in our solar system, with winds that can reach speeds of up to 2,100 kilometers (1,300 miles) per hour. These strong winds are caused by the planet's rapid rotation and its large, thick atmosphere, which is primarily made up of hydrogen, helium, and methane gas.

Fun Fact

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system, and also the closest planet to the sun. One interesting fact about Mercury is that it has a very slow rotation compared to other planets in our solar system, with one day on Mercury taking about 59 Earth days. However, despite its slow rotation, Mercury has a very fast orbital speed around the sun, completing one orbit in just 88 Earth days.

Fun Fact

Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" because it is similar in size, mass, and composition to our planet. However, despite these similarities, Venus has a very different atmosphere than Earth. In fact, Venus has one of the most hostile environments in our solar system, with a thick atmosphere that is mostly made up of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid clouds that trap heat.

Fun Fact

Uranus is the seventh planet from the sun and is often referred to as an "ice giant" because its atmosphere is mostly made up of hydrogen, helium, and methane gas. One interesting fact about Uranus is that it rotates on its side, meaning that its north and south poles are located where other planets have their equators. This is thought to be due to a collision with a massive object early in the planet's history, which knocked it off its original axis.

Fun Fact

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and is the fifth largest moon in the solar system. One interesting fact about the Moon is that it is slowly moving away from Earth at a rate of about 3.8 centimeters (1.5 inches) per year. This is due to the gravitational interaction between the Earth and the Moon, which causes the Moon to transfer some of its energy to the Earth's rotation, gradually slowing it down.
